1. Setting Up the Kanban Board in Jira

  1. Log in to Jira Home and navigate to Projects.
  2. Select your project (e.g., Algoclan Project).
  3. Click on Board → Create Board → Choose Kanban Board.
  4. Select the project and click Create.
  5. Customize board columns to include:
    • Backlog
    • To Do
    • In Progress
    • Code Review
    • Reopen – For tasks requiring rework
    • Done
  6. Enable Story Points and set up estimation fields under Board Settings.

3. Story Points & Timelines

Story points estimate task complexity. For planning, we map these points to approximate timelines:

Story Points Complexity Estimated Timeline
1 Very simple task ~4 hours
2 Simple task ~1 day
3 Moderate task ~1 day 4 hours
5 Complex task ~2 days
8 Very complex task ~3-4 days
13+ Needs splitting Break into smaller tasks

During sprint planning and standup calls, managers use these guidelines to set realistic expectations.

4. Task Workflow & Tracking Measures

  1. Backlog: Ideas and tasks awaiting prioritization.
  2. To Do: Tasks chosen for the current cycle.
  3. In Progress: Tasks currently being developed. Maintain WIP limits (e.g., maximum 3 tasks per developer).
  4. Code Review: Completed tasks awaiting review.
  5. Reopen: Tasks that fail acceptance criteria and require rework. Frequent reopens may negatively impact developer KPIs.
  6. Done: Tasks that have passed review and are complete.

Jira’s time tracking and automated alerts help ensure tasks are updated according to the estimated timelines.

5. Permissions & Roles

Establishing clear permissions is essential for maintaining consistency and accountability. Below are the suggested roles and their permissions:

  • Jira Administrator / Project Administrator: Full permissions to configure boards, workflows, custom fields, and manage user roles.
  • Project Manager / Team Lead: Can assign and adjust story points, manage tasks, modify issue statuses, and set target KPIs.
  • Developer: Can update the status of assigned tasks, log work, and comment on issues. Note: Developers should not alter story point estimates.
  • QA / Tester: Can comment, log defects, and validate tasks without modifying story points or workflow settings.

These roles help ensure that only authorized users can make critical changes—such as assigning story points or adjusting workflows—thus maintaining process integrity.

6. Manager Guidelines & KPI Assignment

Managers are responsible for ensuring task quality and meeting deadlines:

  • Standup Meetings: Discuss tasks with developers and assign story points based on task complexity and timeline estimates.
  • Point Assignment: Only managers or team leads should adjust story points to maintain consistency.
  • KPI Metrics: Monitor:
    • Timeliness (completion within estimated timelines),
    • Quality (number of tasks sent back to Reopen), and
    • Throughput (number of tasks completed per cycle).
  • Reopened Tasks: Frequent reopens will be tracked and may affect a developer’s KPI.

Managers should leverage Jira’s reporting tools to review KPIs during regular performance discussions.

8. Reports, Metrics & Automation

Jira offers various reporting tools to monitor workflow health and efficiency:

  • Control Chart: Track cycle and lead times for tasks.
  • Cumulative Flow Diagram (CFD): Visualize work in progress and identify bottlenecks.
  • Automation Rules: 
    
Trigger: Issue transitioned to "In Progress"
Condition: JQL – status = "In Progress" AND updated <= -5d
Action: Send Slack/Email alert
    Automation ensures timely notifications if tasks remain stagnant.

9. Agile Concepts & Best Practices

Our development approach combines Scrum with continuous development:

  • Daily Standups: Brief meetings to discuss progress and blockers.
  • Sprint Planning: Collaborative review of tasks and estimation of story points.
  • Continuous Integration: Frequent code merges and regular code reviews to maintain quality.
  • Retrospectives: Periodic sessions to discuss improvements.
  • Feedback Loop: Encourage open communication and ongoing adjustments.
