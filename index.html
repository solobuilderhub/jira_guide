1. Setting Up the Kanban Board in Jira

  1. Log in to Jira Home and navigate to Projects.
  2. Select your project (e.g., Algoclan Project).
  3. Click on Board → Create Board → Choose Kanban Board.
  4. Select the project and click Create.
  5. Customize board columns to include:
    • Backlog
    • To Do
    • In Progress
    • Code Review
    • Reopen – For tasks requiring rework
    • Done
  6. Enable Story Points and set up estimation fields under Board Settings.

3. Story Points & Timelines

Story points estimate task complexity. For planning, we map these points to approximate timelines:

Story Points Complexity Estimated Timeline
1 Very simple task ~4 hours
2 Simple task ~1 day
3 Moderate task ~1 day 4 hours
5 Complex task ~2 days
8 Very complex task ~3-4 days
13+ Needs splitting Break into smaller tasks

During sprint planning and standup calls, managers use these guidelines to set realistic expectations.

4. Task Workflow & Tracking Measures

Our workflow is designed for continuous development and transparency:

  1. Backlog: Ideas and tasks awaiting prioritization.
  2. To Do: Tasks chosen for the current cycle.
  3. In Progress: Tasks currently being developed. WIP limits (e.g., max 3 tasks) should be maintained.
  4. Code Review: Completed tasks awaiting review.
  5. Reopen: Tasks that fail acceptance criteria and require rework. Frequent reopening may negatively affect a developer’s KPI.
  6. Done: Tasks that have passed review and are complete.

Jira’s time tracking and automated alerts help ensure tasks are updated according to the estimated timelines.

5. Manager Guidelines & KPI Assignment

Managers play a crucial role in ensuring task quality and meeting deadlines:

  • Standup Meetings: Discuss tasks with developers and assign story points based on complexity and timeline estimates.
  • Point Assignment: Only managers can adjust story points to maintain consistency in estimates.
  • KPI Metrics:
    • Timeliness: Completion of tasks within the estimated timeline.
    • Quality: Number of tasks sent back to the Reopen column.
    • Throughput: Number of tasks completed per cycle.
  • Impact of Reopened Tasks: Tasks that are reopened will be tracked, and frequent reopens may affect the developer’s KPI.

Managers should use Jira’s reporting tools to monitor KPIs and discuss performance in periodic review meetings.

7. Reports, Metrics & Automation

Jira provides various reporting tools to monitor workflow health and efficiency:

  • Control Chart: Track cycle and lead times for tasks.
  • Cumulative Flow Diagram (CFD): Visualize work in progress and identify bottlenecks.
  • Automation Rules: 
    
Trigger: Issue transitioned to "In Progress"
Condition: JQL – status = "In Progress" AND updated <= -5d
Action: Send Slack/Email alert
    Automation ensures timely notifications if tasks remain stagnant.

8. Agile Concepts & Best Practices

Our development approach is a hybrid of Scrum and continuous development:

  • Daily Standups: Brief meetings to discuss progress and blockers.
  • Sprint Planning: Collaborative review of tasks and estimation of story points.
  • Continuous Integration: Frequent code merges and regular code reviews to maintain quality.
  • Retrospectives: Periodic sessions to discuss improvements.
  • Feedback Loop: Encourage open communication and ongoing adjustments.

9. Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of how Algoclan manages its tasks and measures performance using Jira. By following the defined board setup, estimation, and KPI guidelines, all team members will understand what to expect and how to deliver work efficiently.
